# Competitive Analysis: Levy Strategic Design vs. Kaptivate Group

**Date:** April 2026
**Prepared for:** Internal strategy use
**Classification:** Internal / Confidential

---

## Executive Summary

Levy and Kaptivate operate in the same D.C. systems-change ecosystem — workforce development, education, infrastructure, and economic development — but embody fundamentally different models.

**Kaptivate** is a traditional consulting firm (11–50 employees, government procurement vehicles) that delivers programs *to* institutions from the outside. **Levy** is a network of ecosystem actors — founders, institutional decision-makers, and community leaders — that designs interventions *from within* the ecosystem it serves.

This network-native model, combined with scalable interventions like The Upskilling Labs, creates compounding advantages Kaptivate cannot replicate. However, Kaptivate holds structural advantages in government procurement access, track record length, and SDB certifications that Levy must address strategically.

Levy competes on two fronts:
1. **Institutional/government clients** — the same contracts Kaptivate pursues
2. **Individual ecosystem actors** — the founders, decision-makers, and community leaders Levy recruits as members

## Levy's Competitive Advantages

### Ecosystem-Native Network
Levy's members aren't consultants — they're the founders, agency decision-makers, and community leaders who *are* the ecosystem. Interventions are co-designed with the people who will adopt, implement, and spread them. Kaptivate delivers from outside; Levy designs from within.

### Scalable, Distributable Interventions
Programs like The Upskilling Labs are built to replicate across institutions — libraries, universities, agencies. Each deployment strengthens the network and generates new members. Kaptivate's project model starts from scratch each engagement.

### Dual Flywheel
More ecosystem actors in the network → better interventions → more institutional wins → more ecosystem actors join. Kaptivate has no equivalent compounding mechanism.

### Content Moat
Newsletter, executive essays, and monthly briefings create ongoing thought leadership with zero equivalent from Kaptivate. This drives both member acquisition and institutional credibility simultaneously.

### Community as Infrastructure
Builders Breakfasts, Strategy Sprints, and peer relationships create durable, switching-cost connections. Kaptivate's project teams disband when contracts end.

### Brand Sophistication
Minimalist, institutional design signals the quality and exclusivity that attracts both premium members and discerning institutional buyers.

### Multi-Sector Signal
A network spanning startups, NGOs, corporations, and government gives Levy cross-sector intelligence no single consulting firm can match — and that intelligence is exactly what institutional clients need.

## Kaptivate's Advantages & Threats

### Government Procurement Vehicles
OASIS+ and HCaTS give Kaptivate direct access to large federal contracts. **This is the single biggest structural gap** — Levy isn't in the procurement system yet. Until this changes, Kaptivate wins contracts by default regardless of delivery model quality.

### 15+ Years of Track Record
Institutional trust with the Dept. of Education, GSA, and the World Bank takes time to build. Levy's model is newer and less proven to government buyers who value longevity and past performance references.

### Staffing Compliance
Some RFPs require named FTEs and organizational capacity charts. A network model may not satisfy traditional procurement requirements without creative framing (letters of commitment, advisory board structures, ecosystem partnership agreements).

### Minority-Owned/SDB Certifications
Legally mandated set-aside preferences in government procurement that Levy currently cannot access.

### Open Innovation Creates Competing Communities
Prize challenges like Career Z and CTE CHIPS bring together the exact ecosystem actors Levy wants as members — founders, educators, workforce professionals. These people are currently in Kaptivate's orbit, temporarily organized around Kaptivate-run programs.

### Execution Track Record at Scale
$2.5M prize pools, multi-phase national challenges, international contracts — demonstrated ability to run large programs that Levy hasn't yet matched publicly.

## Strategic Recommendations

### 1. Frame the Network as the Product in Institutional Pitches
*"Our network includes the founders, agency leaders, and community organizers your program needs to reach. We don't deliver to the ecosystem — we are the ecosystem."*

This is the core competitive narrative against Kaptivate. Every proposal should make clear that Levy's membership network is the delivery mechanism, not a staffing pool.

### 2. Document The Upskilling Labs as the Scalable Intervention Model
Measurable outcomes, replication playbook, sites deployed, participants served. This is Levy's answer to Kaptivate's prize challenge track record. Show that interventions designed *within* the ecosystem scale faster and stick longer than those imposed from outside.

### 3. Recruit from Kaptivate's Project Alumni
Career Z participants, CTE CHIPS stakeholders, and open innovation cohort members are ecosystem actors who've been temporarily organized by Kaptivate around the exact topics Levy serves. They should be permanent Levy members. Target these cohorts with Insider-tier messaging.

### 4. Own the Thought Leadership Gap
Kaptivate executes programs but publishes nothing about methodology, frameworks, or lessons learned. Levy should publish on open innovation design, workforce development strategy, and scalable interventions. Position Levy as the strategic intelligence layer; Kaptivate as the implementation vendor.

### 5. Build Procurement Readiness Strategically
SAM.gov registration and relevant certifications open the door. Frame the network model in procurement-friendly language — letters of commitment from named ecosystem actors, advisory board structures, institutional partnership MOUs — without compromising the actual model.

### 6. Make the Network Visible in Proposals
Named ecosystem actors (with permission) who will participate, adopt, advise, or serve as pilot sites. This turns Levy's membership into a tangible asset that procurement officers can evaluate and that Kaptivate cannot match.

### 7. Accelerate Content as a Dual-Use Asset
Every essay, briefing, and Upskilling Labs recap simultaneously acquires new members AND builds the credibility profile needed for institutional contracts. Double the publication cadence — this is Levy's widest competitive moat.

### 8. Design Builder Tier Around Ecosystem Activation
Builders should be the most active ecosystem actors — the ones who show up in proposals, pilot interventions, bring their institutions into the network, and amplify Levy's credibility through their own professional standing.

## Opportunities

- **Kaptivate's project alumni as warm member pipeline** — they're already organized around the right topics and have experienced the limitations of temporary, project-based community
- **Open innovation methodology as a content category** — no one in the D.C. ecosystem publishes on prize challenge design and open innovation strategy; Levy's Institute can own this
- **Scalable intervention licensing** — The Upskilling Labs model deployed through partner institutions (libraries, universities, agencies) as a network growth multiplier
- **Cross-sector network intelligence** — a unique value proposition in government proposals that no single consulting firm can replicate
- **Partnership model for large contracts** — Levy's ecosystem access + a delivery partner's procurement infrastructure for contracts too large to pursue alone
